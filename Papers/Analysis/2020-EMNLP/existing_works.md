# Existing Works

## Previous Approaches

- Prior computational work has focused mainly on simile detection and analysis rather than generation.
- Simile detection studies include annotation and recognition of similes in user-generated and literary text.
- Earlier metaphor generation methods relied on templates, heuristics, lexical databases, or word embeddings.
- More recent metaphor generation work used neural models and seq2seq masking approaches.

## Related Research

- Simile detection and implicit property inference: Niculae and Danescu-Niculescu-Mizil (2014), Qadir et al. (2015, 2016), Liu et al. (2018), Zeng et al. (2019).
- Metaphor generation: Abe et al. (2006), Terai and Nakagawa (2010), Gero and Chilton (2019), Gagliano et al. (2016), Young (1987), Yu and Wan (2019), Stowe et al. (2020).
- Style transfer and seq2seq generation methods: Shen et al. (2017), Fu et al. (2017), Li et al. (2018), Sudhakar et al. (2019).

## Gaps in Existing Solutions

- Existing metaphor generation work does not directly address simile generation.
- Earlier approaches often do not preserve contextual relevance between the literal input and the generated figure of speech.
- There was no large-scale parallel corpus for simile generation before this work.
- The paper argues that simile generation requires explicit handling of commonsense property mappings, which earlier systems lack.
